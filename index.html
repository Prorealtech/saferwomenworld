OUR MISSION

We envision a transformative future to curate a captivating safe space where raw talent meets opportunity, showcasing the brilliance of women from diverse backgrounds. We stand as a beacon of support, breaking stereotypes and fostering a vibrant community. We aspire to redefine standards, providing women with the necessary opportunities to showcase their aptitude and expertise. Through this commitment, we seek to contribute to a safer world by uplifting communities to navigate challenges effectively, celebrate strength, creativity, and resilience of women, encouraging them to conquer challenges and soar to remarkable heights. Our ultimate goal is to be instrumental in building a safer world, where the brilliance of every woman shines brightly, contributing to a more equitable and secure future for all.

ABOUT US

Influenced by the cause of safeguarding his daughter from the evils of the society that have been plaguing women, our founder – Sambit Ray, has envisioned a transformative initiative. At 'Safer Women World', he aspires to create a realm where women receive training, guidance, and the means to showcase and monetize their inherent talents. Our founder envisions to create an entire ecosystem fostering independence, freedom, and empowerment, eliminating the need for external support. Sambit has won multiple accolades as an award-winning entrepreneur and his affiliations with esteemed organizations like IFTA(Hollywood), OTT.x, and the American Film Institute attest to his commitment. He is backed with decades of technology and marketing experience with a flair for creative writing and directing. ‘Safer Women World’, now a global platform, thrives on support, coordination, communication, and cooperation from women worldwide.

Soviet Peasants

Women have been an integral part of agricultural labor, they have bore the weight of both household and fieldwork. Despite societal constraints, they exhibited remarkable resilience, and have embodied the spirit of collective farming initiatives. Their contributions to Soviet agriculture were immense, yet often overlooked. Their stories reflect both struggle and steadfast determination in shaping the modern world.
